⚠️ When your friend is actually (kind of) right

  • Very low base odds or B = 1: when the probability of winning is tiny, the naive "×A/B" estimate is nearly accurate — the gap only becomes meaningful at moderate-to-high base probabilities, or when B is already large.
  • Without replacement with X = 1: if there's only one winning outcome, drawing more times is exactly linear. Going from 1 to 3 draws really is exactly 3× as likely. Don't argue that one.
